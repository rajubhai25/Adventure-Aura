Panchavati

Panchavati, meaning "five banyan trees" in Sanskrit, refers to a sacred grove or forest associated with the Hindu epic Ramayana. It's believed that Lord Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana resided there during their exile. Panchavati is also a specific location near Nashik, Maharashtra, India, known for its historical and religious significance.
