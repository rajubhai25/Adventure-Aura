Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial, located in Kolkata, is a grand white marble building built to honor Queen Victoria. Designed in a blend of British and Mughal architectural styles, it serves as a museum and memorial, housing artifacts and showcasing India's history under the British Empire.

Dakshineswar Temple

Dakshineswar Kali Temple, a prominent Hindu temple, is located in Dakshineswar, a town near Kolkata, India. It is a sacred site dedicated to the goddess Kali and is known for its traditional "nava-ratna" (nine-spire) architectural style. The temple was founded in 1855 by Rani Rashmoni, a wealthy and pious woman, who had a vision of building a temple for Kali. The temple complex also features smaller shrines dedicated to other Hindu deities, including Shiva.

Howrah Bridge

The Howrah Bridge, also known as the Rabindra Setu, is a landmark cantilever bridge in Kolkata, India, spanning the Hooghly River. It connects Howrah and Kolkata, serving as a vital transportation route for vehicles and pedestrians. The bridge is noted for its riveted steel construction and its role as a symbolic link between the two cities.
