Echo Point

An "Echo Point" is a location, often a hill or valley, where shouting or making a loud sound creates a noticeable echo or repetition of the sound. This phenomenon occurs due to sound waves bouncing off surrounding surfaces (like hills or cliffs) and returning to the listener, creating a delayed version of the original sound.

Mattupetty Dam

Mattupetty Dam, located near Munnar in Kerala, India, is a concrete gravity dam built for water storage and hydroelectricity generation. It's a popular tourist spot, known for its scenic beauty and the large reservoir, Mattupetty Lake, which offers boating and wildlife viewing opportunities. The dam was constructed in the late 1940s and is vital for the region's power supply and water conservation.

Eravikulam National Park

Eravikulam National Park, also known as Rajamala, is a 97 sq km park in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India. It's renowned for being a habitat for the Nilgiri Tahr, an endangered mountain goat, and the Neelakurinji flower, which blooms once every 12 years. The park also contains Anamudi, the highest peak in South India.
