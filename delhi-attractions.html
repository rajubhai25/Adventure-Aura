Must-Visit Attractions in Delhi

India Gate

The India Gate is a war memorial located near the Rajpath on the eastern edge of the "ceremonial axis" of New Delhi

Red Fort

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a historical fort in Old Delhi, India, and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in the 17th century, it served as the main residence of the Mughal Emperors. The fort is renowned for its stunning red sandstone walls and intricate marble structures, reflecting the peak of Mughal architecture.

Qutub Minar

The Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the tallest brick minaret in the world. Located in Delhi, India, it's part of the larger Qutub Complex and stands at 72.5 meters (237.8 feet) high. Construction began in 1199 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, the founder of the Delhi Sultanate, and it's known for its intricate carvings and projecting balconies.
